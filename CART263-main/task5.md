For Task 5, I got to check out all the different projects my classmates made, and it was really cool to see how everyone approached things in their own way. Each project had its own vibe, and it was interesting to see how different ideas came together in this little showcase.
Leah and Norah made a photo gallery inspired by the Apple photo gallery with all those simple, kind of silly effects. They put their own spin on it and made it into a small website where you could click through the pictures. The gallery also lets you save your photos and look at them directly in the site, which made it feel more useful instead of just being something to look at. They chose a Windows XP theme, which was kind of ironic considering the Apple inspiration behind it. That choice added some personality and humor to the project. Overall, it was really satisfying to see their work come together, and the site felt clean, playful, and well put together.
Phil made a really satisfying interactive mouse game where a swarm of fish tries to smother the cursor only when the mouse is moving. I really liked what he came up with because it was clean, fun, and kind of hypnotizing to play with. One thing I noticed is that he used divs for the fish while I used canvas in my project, which made his version feel smooth in a different way. I also want to give him props because his fish look way better than mine, not that I am comparing, but it is obvious. If I could, I would use some of his code for my worm game because his approach is just so effective. Overall, it was super engaging and really fun to interact with.
Emile made a small interactive site where you can log your bike. You can add the brand and all the accessories or parts, which I think is really neat. As someone who has followed Emile for a bit, I can definitely see his personality in the project. It feels like something he would naturally make. I was impressed by the flow of his work too, nothing was flashy and it is really easy on the eyes. While I am not a crazy biker, I had my dad log his bike and he said it was actually really helpful to have all the info in one place neatly organized. Overall, it is simple but effective and a fun way to interact with something you care about.
Looking at all these projects made me realize how different approaches to interaction can make something really engaging. From the playful photo gallery to the hypnotizing fish game to the smooth and organized bike logger, each project had its own style and personality. It was cool to see how my classmates’ creativity and attention to detail shaped the way their projects worked and felt. Seeing them all together made me appreciate not just the coding but also the thought and personality behind each one.
